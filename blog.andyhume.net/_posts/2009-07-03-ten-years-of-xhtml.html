This week, the W3C announced that work is stopping on XHTML 2.

It always had to be evolutionary and not revolutionary. Revolution comes at a time when you're stuck.

Revolution or evolution

The mistake that was made, and that many people realised at the time, was that the W3C were trying to precipitate revolution when they were not empowered to do so.

HTML serialized as XML is a worthy goal, and one that continues with XHTML 5 The W3C aren't in a position to drive revolution like that. Certainly not with a technology as well established and successful as HTML.
